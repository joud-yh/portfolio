Wattواط

A smart energy-tracking app that turns invisible kilowatts into something Saudi households can see, understand, and act on — one device at a time.

⚡
Overview

Make energy visible· نبذة

Saudi homes are filling up with smart devices — air conditioners, washing machines, smart lighting — but most people have no idea which one is eating their bill alive. Watt was built to close that gap.

The premise

A single window into a whole home. Real-time tracking per device, daily and weekly reports, and personalized tips — all delivered in calm, friendly Arabic. The user doesn't need to be a power engineer. They just need to see what's using what, and decide.

The result is an app that treats energy data the way fitness apps treat steps: visible, actionable, and a little satisfying.

The shift

From invisible to actionable

Saudi consumption rose 9.3% between July 2023 and July 2024, and bills followed. Yet per-device usage stays hidden inside one monthly number — users can't connect a behavior to a cost.

The problem

Energy is invisible until the bill arrives

Households use more electricity each year, but the breakdown is hidden inside one monthly number. Users can't connect a behavior to a cost — so they can't change it.

Watt's promise

See every device. Get a small nudge.

Watt breaks the bill into per-device, real-time usage and pairs each device with a one-line tip — turning a vague worry into a clear choice.

Objectives

Three things Watt has to do

These three objectives shaped every flow, every screen, and every tip in the app.

01

Manage smart device consumption

Track AC, washing machine, smart lighting in real time and give actionable tips — optimal AC temp, smart scheduling, what to unplug.

02

Raise awareness, gently

Help people see why a device is heavy on the grid and what changing one habit can save — without lecturing or shaming.

03

Deliver clear daily & weekly reports

Reports per device and for the whole home — visual, scannable, with charts that read in two seconds and tips that read in five.

User research

What 5 households told us

We ran qualitative interviews with five Saudi residential users. Three insights kept surfacing across every conversation.

Insight 01

Bills are rising, devices are multiplying

Every participant noticed their bill climb after adding ACs, washing machines, ovens. They felt the change but couldn't trace its source.

Insight 02

Smart devices ≠ smart users

Opinions split. Some saw smart devices as energy-saving; others felt they used more. Almost none used any app to manage them.

Insight 03

They wanted visibility, not lectures

Users asked for graphs, daily/weekly breakdowns, and tailored tips per device — not generic eco advice or shaming alerts.

Challenges

What was hard· التحديات

01

Making numbers feel like a story

A wall of watts and percentages is data nobody reads. We pushed everything through curved charts, min/avg/max bands, and color-coded device icons — so a glance tells the whole story.

Data as glance
02

Tips that respect the user

Energy advice has a bad habit of sounding like a parent. Every tip in Watt was written short, specific, and in second-person Arabic — "استخدم الإضاءة الطبيعية", not "يجب عليك تقليل الاستهلاك".

Tone, not lectures
03

Onboarding without onboarding

Adding a device is a friction point — scanning a barcode, joining Wi-Fi, naming a room. We compressed it into a four-tap flow with a QR scanner as the default and a manual code as fallback.

Friction reduction
04

From "My Devices" to "My Rooms"

Early testers had trouble locating a specific device. We restructured the home around rooms, not device categories — so users navigate the way they actually think about their home.

Mental model shift
