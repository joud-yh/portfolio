Rehlaaرحلة

السفر بأسلوب سعودي

A personalized trip-planning app for Saudi travelers — letting them shape their own itinerary by venue, restaurant, and budget, with rewards that grow with every journey.

🧭
Overview

Travel, but on your own terms· نبذة

Most travel apps tell you where to go. Rehlaa was built for a simple insight: travelers want to shape the trip themselves — pick the venues, the restaurants, the activities — all inside a budget they control.

The premise

A Saudi-first trip-planning app built around four familiar tabs — Home, Search, My Trips, and Profile — so navigation stays effortless. Underneath, a system of offers, reward points, and live customization keeps users planning and coming back.

Visually it leans into Saudi identity: deep navy, warm magenta-to-orange gradients, and iconic destinations like Historic Jeddah, Diriyah, and AlUla — so every trip feels rooted in place, not generic.

  • Type Academic app project
  • Year 2024 — 2025
  • Platform iOS · mobile-first
  • Language Arabic-first (RTL)

Target user

Who Rehlaa is for

Saudi travelers planning local and cultural trips inside the Kingdom — anyone who wants a tailored itinerary instead of a fixed package.

🧳

Saudi domestic travelers

Locals planning weekend escapes or culture trips inside KSA. They want flexible bookings, clear pricing, social proof from other travelers, and a familiar Arabic interface that respects how they actually search and pay.

UX techniques

How the interface guides the user

Nine design techniques mapped to real screens — each one chosen to make the trip feel easier to plan, safer to book, and more satisfying to complete.

Familiarity

Search bar + bottom nav

A search bar on top and a four-tab nav at the bottom — patterns travelers already know from every other app, so they don't have to learn Rehlaa.

Motivation

Points wallet

Every booking earns points, shown prominently in the profile and at checkout — turning a one-time trip into a reason to come back.

Social acceptance

Visitors, bookings & share

The Jeddah trip page surfaces how many people viewed and booked it, plus a quick share button — bringing the crowd into the decision.

Facilitator

One-tap login

Sign in with Google, Apple, or Facebook — removing the friction of typing emails and passwords before the first plan.

Loss aversion

Original price struck through

Showing the old price crossed out next to the new one frames the saving as something the user would lose by not booking now.

Emotional

Calm Saudi palette

Backgrounds and hero images use warm, harmonious colors — no clutter, no harsh red alerts — so planning feels like a soft moment, not a sales push.

Ability

Customize your itinerary

On the customize page, travelers add or remove destinations themselves and see the price shift live — control that fits their actual budget.

Control

Flexible filters

The search page lets users narrow trips by type — cultural, historical, sea — so they reach the right trip without endless scrolling.

Spark

Prominent "Book now"

A high-contrast gradient CTA at the bottom of the customize page makes the final action obvious and inviting at the right moment.

Persuasion design

Quiet persuasion, not pressure

Twelve persuasion patterns layered into the flow — each one anchored to a specific screen, written in the user's voice, not the app's.

Conformity

"Most popular" row

Surfacing top-booked trips on the home page nudges hesitant users toward what the crowd already trusts.

Social validation

Star ratings everywhere

Ratings appear both in search results and on each trip page — confidence borrowed from past travelers.

Visual influence

Hero imagery + bold offers

Striking destination photography and a bold discount tag turn an offer into something you feel before you read.

Commitment

Points across every step

Your point balance follows you from profile, to order details, to payment — making each booking a step toward the next reward.

Reciprocity

Welcome discount

New users land with a 20% code already in their wallet — a small gift before they've done anything.

Artifacts

Consistent icon system

The same icon family across login, nav, and detail pages keeps the visual grammar one continuous voice.

Spin

"وسّع آفاقك"

Reframing offers as a phrase about expanding horizons makes the section feel like inspiration, not a sales banner.

Tradition

Heritage hero imagery

Photos of Historic Jeddah and AlUla anchor trips in cultural memory — the trip feels meaningful before it's even booked.

Gain frame

"Save up to 20%"

The booking banner emphasizes what the user gains — framing the action around money saved, not money spent.

Loss frame

"Don't miss limited offers"

Right above the final pay button, urgency is named directly — a gentle push when the user is already convinced.

Central route

Customize with full info

The customize screen shows every destination, every benefit, and live price — letting users decide on substance, not impulse.

Slogan

"السفر بأسلوب سعودي"

The splash slogan ties the app's identity to a feeling — travel done the Saudi way, in your own style.

Challenges

What was hard· التحديات

01

Balancing persuasion with respect

Persuasive techniques can easily tip into manipulative. We had to choose patterns that nudge — popularity, savings, social proof — without surrounding the user with red countdowns and shouting CTAs.

Calm by design
02

Arabic-first, not Arabic-translated

Every piece of copy was written in Arabic from the start. The visual hierarchy had to support RTL reading without feeling like a mirrored Western app.

Native voice
03

Making customization feel light

Letting users build their own itinerary can become overwhelming fast. We kept the customize page to a flat list of toggleable destinations and a single live price — the complexity stays under the hood.

Hidden complexity
04

Saudi identity without cliché

Heritage imagery had to feel like real destinations, not folkloric decoration. We let the photography do the cultural work, while the UI stayed quiet and modern.

Cultural rooting
