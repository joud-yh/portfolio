How I Work

My process blends Design Thinking's empathy with the structure of the Double Diamond — and a bias toward shipping fast. Every project moves through these six moments, sometimes looping back when users surprise me.

01

Empathize

Listen first. Interview users, observe behavior, sit with their frustration before sketching anything.

"Why do they really need this?"
02

Define

Frame the real problem. Turn messy research into one sharp problem statement everyone agrees on.

"What are we actually solving?"
03

Ideate

Sketch wide before going deep. Many options on paper, then converge on the strongest direction.

"What if we tried it differently?"
04

Prototype

Build to learn, not to impress. Low-fi first, then high-fi only when the flow is proven.

"Let's make it real enough to test."
05

Test

Watch real users break it. Their confusion is the most honest feedback I'll ever get.

"Where did they hesitate?"
06

Ship

Don't polish forever. Hand off cleanly, work with devs, and learn from what real users do next.

"Done is better than perfect."
